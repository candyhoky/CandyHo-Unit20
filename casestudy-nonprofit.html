Website Redesign for
Australian Koala Foundation

Sector

Nonprofit, Endangered Animals Awareness

Goals

Improve the user experience of making a donation to increase the rate of donations received
Enhance the information architecture of the homepage and navigation bar
Advocate for the protection of koalas in a visually compelling and responsive website design.

Collaboration

Asrufa, Ell and Nathan

My Role

UX / UI Designer

Project Time

3 Weeks

Deliverables

Research Results, style guide, wireframes, prototypes for desktop and mobile version

Tools

Miro, Figma, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop
PHASE 1

User Research

Objectives

To identify user's pain point when navigating through the current website and making a donation.
To understand user's motivations and expectations when visting a non-profit website.

Methodology

Conducted guerilla testing of the existing website with five people;
Surveyed 24 people between the ages of 22 and 57;
Conducted a card sorting exercise using existing primary and secondary pages;
Redlining and heristic evaluation of the homepage and donation pages;
Conducted a competitor analysis of five other non-profit websites.

Current website

PHASE 2

Definition & Synthesis

Key Findings

The current website is disorderly, difficult to navigate and does not inspire users to contribute;
Over 90% of users surveyed would rather provide a financial contribution;
Users seek trust, transparency and purpose when visiting non profit websites;
Donation pages of competitors are much more simplier and reduce user choice overload.

Competitor Analysis

PHASE 3

Definition & Synthesis

How might we improve the donation process so users feel encouraged to donate and assured that their contribution will be used in supporting the cause directly.

PHASE 4

Prototyping &
Responsive Design

Search, Shop and Cart buttons hidden behind hamburger icon on tablet and mobile.
Top navigation will be hidden inside the hamburger menu on mobile version.
Hero banner text and button seperates from image on mobile.
Cards stacked in one column on mobile.

PHASE 5

User Testing & Outcomes

Key Findings

Users do not understand the progress bar here. May be it need more indication, eg. number of steps, and differientation between states.
A back button is also added as users can always go back and edit if they wish to.
Buttons also renamed to indicate different stages of the progress. For example: Confirm to Donate.
Users are not sure why the donation stars are in different colours and not sure if there’s more of those cards as it got chopped off at the end of the page. Also suggested may be some animation would be nice to included.

Prototype iteration

PHASE 6

Conclusion and Future Opportunities

Further work is required to optimise features such as the shop, adopt a koala, and plant a tree. Time constraints meant our focus was limited to homepage and donation pages.

The organisation was unable to assist with our project. This meant we had to assume which features and information should be priortised.

Further testing and iteration is required as the site expandes. For example, it is unclear whether the shop and cart icon in the nav bar are optimally positioned.
